Portraiture: Family,
Lifestyle & Couples

Real-life portraiture for photographers ready to find their niche and shoot with consistency.

About this course
Portraits don't happen in a vacuum.

Real-world portrait sessions are unpredictable. Kids don't follow directions. Couples freeze up. Families argue in the parking lot before you've even started. The light is wrong, the location is mediocre, and the session is half over before everyone relaxes. This course is built for that reality.

Through a mix of video lessons, written content, and interactive exercises, you'll build a working system for directing subjects, reading light, and finding the images worth keeping within the chaos of a real session. The goal is to develop enough confidence and consistency that your own style can emerge, and to identify the niche within portraiture that genuinely suits you.

Families, lifestyle couples, engagements, or all three: this course gives you the tools to approach each shoot with intention and walk away with images you're proud of.

What you'll cover
Inside the course.
Directing without directing
How to pose and prompt families, couples, and children in a way that feels natural. Techniques for getting genuine reactions rather than stiff, posed results.
Light in the real world
Working with natural light in unpredictable environments: open shade, harsh sun, backlight, indoor window light. How to find it, use it, and work around it when it's not cooperating.
When sessions go sideways
Managing the session when the plan falls apart. Strategies for staying calm, keeping subjects engaged, and recovering the shoot when nothing is going as expected.
Finding your niche
Identifying where within portrait and lifestyle photography you do your best work. How to build toward a consistent body of work that reflects your own style.
