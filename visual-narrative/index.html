Visual Narrative

Intentional photography that shows the world from your point of view.

About this course
Technical skill gets you in the door. Intention is what makes people look twice.

Most photographers spend years developing their technical skills: exposure, focus, composition. Then they hit a wall where technically solid images feel flat. The missing piece is almost always narrative: a sense that the photographer had something to say, and that the image is saying it.

This course works through what intentional, story-driven photography actually means in practice. Through video lessons, written content, and interactive exercises, you'll develop a framework for approaching any subject with a point of view, build work that holds together as a series rather than a collection of individual shots, and find a visual voice that's yours rather than an imitation of whoever you've been studying.

The principles apply across genres. Portraits, street, landscape, documentary: the approach to narrative intent is the same. If you're making photographs and want them to mean something, this is for you.

What you'll cover
Inside the course.
The principles of story-driven photography
What separates a technically good image from one that communicates something. How to approach a subject with a question rather than just a camera.
Sequencing a body of work
How to take a set of individual images and build them into something cohesive. Pacing, edit decisions, and how sequence shapes meaning.
Finding a personal visual voice
What your visual voice actually is and how to develop it deliberately. How to study influences without ending up as a copy of them.
Shooting with narrative intent
Applying these principles in the field across any subject or genre. How to pre-visualize, stay flexible, and make photographs that reflect a consistent point of view.
